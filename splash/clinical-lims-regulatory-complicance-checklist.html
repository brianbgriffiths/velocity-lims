Major Regulatory Bodies for Clinical Laboratories

Clinical laboratories must comply with various regulatory frameworks depending on their location, scope, and accreditation goals. Velocity LIMS is designed to support compliance workflows across multiple regulatory environments.

🇺🇸 United States

  • FDA (Food and Drug Administration) - Medical device regulations, 21 CFR Part 820 (Quality System Regulation), 21 CFR Part 11 (Electronic Records)
  • CLIA (Clinical Laboratory Improvement Amendments) - Quality standards for laboratory testing, personnel requirements, quality control
  • CAP (College of American Pathologists) - Laboratory accreditation program, proficiency testing, quality assurance standards
  • CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) - CLIA oversight, reimbursement requirements
  • CDC (Centers for Disease Control) - Public health reporting, infectious disease surveillance
  • OSHA (Occupational Safety and Health Administration) - Laboratory safety standards, bloodborne pathogen protocols

🇪🇺 European Union

  • ISO 15189 - Medical laboratories requirements for quality and competence
  • IVDR (In Vitro Diagnostic Regulation) - EU Regulation 2017/746 for diagnostic devices
  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) - Patient data privacy and protection
  • EMA (European Medicines Agency) - Clinical trial laboratory standards

🇨🇦 Canada

  • Health Canada - Medical device licensing, laboratory standards
  • CSMLS (Canadian Society for Medical Laboratory Science) - Professional certification and standards
  • ISO 15189 - Laboratory quality management systems (adopted by provinces)

🇦🇺 Australia

  • TGA (Therapeutic Goods Administration) - Medical device regulation
  • NATA (National Association of Testing Authorities) - Laboratory accreditation using ISO 15189
  • RACGP (Royal Australian College of General Practitioners) - Point-of-care testing standards

🇯🇵 Japan

  • PMDA (Pharmaceuticals and Medical Devices Agency) - Medical device approval and regulation
  • JCCLS (Japanese Committee for Clinical Laboratory Standards) - Laboratory standardization

🌍 International Standards

  • ISO 15189 - Medical laboratories — Requirements for quality and competence
  • ISO/IEC 17025 - General requirements for testing and calibration laboratories
  • ISO 13485 - Medical devices quality management systems
  • ISO 27001 - Information security management systems
  • GLP (Good Laboratory Practice) - OECD guidelines for non-clinical laboratory studies
  • GCP (Good Clinical Practice) - ICH E6 guidelines for clinical trials

Key Compliance Implementation Areas

Data Integrity & 21 CFR Part 11

Velocity Provides: Electronic records management, audit trails, electronic signatures, access controls, and data backup systems.

You Must Configure: Validation protocols, user training programs, disaster recovery procedures, and formal documentation of all validation activities.

Quality Management Systems

Velocity Provides: Workflow management, deviation tracking capabilities, CAPA workflow framework, and document version control.

You Must Configure: Quality manual documentation, risk assessment procedures, management review processes, and corrective action protocols.

Personnel & Training

Velocity Provides: User role management, permission assignments, and audit trail of user activities.

You Must Configure: Competency assessment programs, training record maintenance, continuing education tracking, and personnel qualification documentation.

Equipment & Calibration

Velocity Provides: Equipment tracking, maintenance scheduling framework, and calibration record storage.

You Must Configure: Calibration procedures, maintenance schedules, equipment qualification protocols, and performance verification programs.

Typical Implementation Timeline: Plan 6-18 months for full regulatory compliance implementation, including system validation, staff training, procedure development, and regulatory inspection preparation.

Implementation Recommendations

  1. Regulatory Consultation - Engage qualified consultants familiar with your specific regulatory requirements
  2. Gap Analysis - Conduct thorough assessment of current processes vs. regulatory requirements
  3. Validation Planning - Develop comprehensive validation master plan and protocols
  4. Pilot Implementation - Start with non-critical processes to test workflows and training
  5. Staff Training - Implement comprehensive training programs before go-live
  6. Documentation - Prepare all required SOPs, policies, and validation documents
  7. Inspection Readiness - Conduct mock inspections and prepare response procedures
